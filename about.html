history of pdk sri satok

The Community-Based Rehabilitation Organization (PDK) Sri Satok is a training center aimed at individuals with disabilities (special needs). This center was established in July 2015 and began full operations in September 2015. Initially, the funding for PDK Sri Satok was entirely provided by the Petra Jaya Welfare Association (PKPJ).

On April 13, 2016, PDK Sri Satok was officially recognized as an entity under the Department of Social Welfare, giving the center formal recognition as part of the national welfare service network. Additionally, PDK Sri Satok was officially registered with the Registrar of Societies (ROS) in 2018, further enhancing the organization's legitimacy and recognition.

    WHAT IS CBR?
    According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the Community-Based Rehabilitation (CBR) program is defined as a strategy within community development for the rehabilitation, equalization of opportunities, and integration of persons with disabilities (PWDs). The CBR program is implemented through the direct involvement of the community, and active participation of PWDs, their families, and local communities, and is supported by health, education, vocational, and social services while utilizing local resources.

    The CBR program focuses on PWDs who require basic rehabilitation and training in terms of movement, speech, special education, and skills for daily living activities. The program is open to all PWDs or any type of disability and caters to all age groups. The approach is through integrated collaboration from various sectors in planning and providing the necessary services and facilities. CBR also serves as an alternative to institutional rehabilitation.

    CBR provides rehabilitation/training to PWDs categorized into 7 types, namely:
    1. Learning Disabilities
    2. Hearing Impairment
    3. Visual Impairment
    4. Speech Impairment
    5. Physical Disabilities
    6. Mental Disabilities
    7. Multiple Disabilities

     our vision, mission, goals & objectives

    — VISION
    It is empowering individuals with disabilities (special needs) and showcasing them as capable members of society in various fields.

    — MISSION
    To become a center that offers the best services and facilities to the disabled community (special needs).

    — GOALS
    The main goals of establishing PDK, aside from conducting rehabilitation and training activities, include: Providing early rehabilitation services and early training (Early Intervention) and Serving as a reference and information center for the community and the general public. Providing rehabilitation, training, and education enhancing self-reliance among PWDs Improving the vocational skills of PWDs

    — OBJECTIVES       
    1. Providing personal support to families with children with disabilities   
    2. Bringing rehabilitation and early intervention services closer to PWDs at the local community level    
    3. Ensuring full participation and social integration of PWDs within the community  
    4. Reducing long-term placements in rehabilitation institutions that isolate PWDs from their families and community
    5. Providing opportunities for PWDs to develop and utilize their existing skills  
    6. Assisting in creating a caring society that is sensitive to the needs of PWDs
