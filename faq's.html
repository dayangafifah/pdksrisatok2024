According to the World Health Organization (WHO), the Community-Based Rehabilitation (CBR) program is defined as a strategy within community development for the rehabilitation, recovery, or equalization of opportunities and integration of Persons with Disabilities (PWD). The CBR program is implemented through direct involvement from the community, active participation of PWD, their families, and the local community, and is supported by health, education, vocational, and social services, utilizing local resources.

The Sri Satok Community-Based Rehabilitation (CBR) Organization is a training center for individuals with disabilities (special needs). Established in July 2015 and fully operational from September 2015, PDK Satok was fully funded by the Petra Jaya Welfare Association (PKPJ) before being officially recognized as an entity under the Department of Social Welfare on April 13, 2016.

PDK Sri Satok was officially registered as an organization with the Registrar of Societies (ROS) in 2018.

- Provide personal support to families with children with disabilities
- Bring rehabilitation services and early intervention for PWD to the local community level
- Ensure full participation and social integration of PWD in the community
- Reduce long-term placement in rehabilitation institutions that isolate PWD from their families and community
- Provide opportunities for PWD to develop and use their skills
- Help create a caring society that is sensitive to the needs of PWD-friendly environments

The PDK program focuses on PWD who require rehabilitation and basic training in terms of movement, speech, special education, and skills for daily life activities. The program is open to all PWD or any type of disability and accepts all age groups. The approach taken is through integrated cooperation from various sectors in planning and providing the necessary services and facilities. PDK is also one of the alternatives to community-based rehabilitation (CBR).

PDK provides rehabilitation/training to PWD categorized into 7 types:
- Learning Disabilities
- Hearing Impairment
- Visual Impairment
- Speech Impairment
- Physical Disability
- Mental Disability
- Multiple Disabilities
